Lean by design

We are a student-founded team keeping costs low. No office. No full-time salaries in year one. Contract developers, free-tier tools where possible, and marketing spend that scales with revenue. At ~£8,300/month, we need just 1,845 paying subscribers at £4.50 blended ARPU to break even — achievable within eight months of launch.

£

How we calculate blended ARPU of £4.50

Blended ARPU (average revenue per user) reflects the weighted average across all paying tiers. We estimate 60% of subscribers will be students at £2.99/month, 30% will be standard Pro users at £4.99/month, and 10% will be Family plan users at £8.99/month. That gives us: (0.6 × £2.99) + (0.3 × £4.99) + (0.1 × £8.99) = £1.79 + £1.50 + £0.90 = £4.19, rounded up to £4.50 to account for annual plan uplift and reduced churn on longer commitments.

Revenue streams
Three ways SnapBite generates income
💳

Stream 1: Subscriptions (primary)

Pro at £4.99/month (£2.99 for students) and Family at £8.99/month. Subscriptions are our core revenue — predictable, recurring, and growing with every new user.

🤝

Stream 2: Partner commissions

Revenue share with Too Good To Go on every bag redeemed. Commission from Ocado on every order placed through the app. Affiliate fees from supermarket meal kits.

🎟️

Stream 3: Coupons and brands

Food brands pay to feature in our weekly coupon drops. Because we know what our users eat, we can offer highly targeted placements — worth more than generic ads.

📈

Beyond break-even

At 5,000 paying users (month 9–10), MRR hits £22,500 — more than double operating costs. Partnership income and coupons add another 15–20% on top. This is a business that scales.
